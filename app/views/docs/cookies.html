Cookies

Cookies are small files put onto your device when you visit and browse a website. We use cookies to make the service work and collect information about how you use our service. You will normally see a message on the site before we store a cookie on your computer.

Cookies aren't used to identify you. You can delete cookies from your device. Find out how to manage cookies.

The cookies we use

Introductory message cookie

When you first visit these pages, you may see a cookie message appear at the top of the screen. Once you’ve seen the message, we store a cookie on your device so your browser does not show it again.

Google Analytics cookies

We use Google Analytics to collect data about how you use the service. This information helps us to improve our service.

Google Analytics stores anonymised information about:

  • how you got to the service
  • the pages you visit on the service and how long you spend on them
  • what you click on while you’re visiting the site

Cookie name What it does Expires

Google doesn’t collect or store your personal information, for example your name or address, so this information can’t be used to identify who you are. We don’t allow Google to use or share our analytics data.
