Privacy notice

These are the standards you can expect from the Environment Agency when we collect, hold or use your personal information when you use our service.

We do this in line with data protection law, including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the Data Protection Act (DPA) 2018.

Who we are

The Environment Agency is the data controller for the Flood Warning System in England. Our personal information charter explains your rights and how we deal with your personal information.

Natural Resources Wales use the same system and are the data controller if you ask for a service in Wales. To read how Natural Resources Wales uses your personal information go to naturalresources.wales/privacy.

What data we need

If you register with us, the personal data we collect about you includes:

  • name
  • contact details for receiving warnings and correspondence
  • address or locations of interest for which you would like to receive warnings

What we do with your personal data

We may use your personal information to:

  • send you the warnings you have signed up for
  • send you a small number of service announcements or administrative messages
  • help emergency services and local councils respond to flooding
  • help with our work on flood warning and flood risk management

Your information is stored and processed securely on servers in the UK.

How we get data from phone companies

We are allowed to process your personal data because we have a legal duty under the Civil Contingencies Act to maintain arrangements to warn people who may be at risk of flooding. The lawful basis for processing your personal data is to perform a task in the public interest that is set out in law.

We work with phone companies to obtain phone numbers for addresses where there is no customer registered for the flood warning service. We tell phone companies the unregistered addresses at risk of flooding. They send us a list of the phone numbers registered to those addresses. They do not give us the names or addresses associated with those numbers.

Your phone number will remain on our system for as long as it remains on the list of numbers provided to us for this purpose.

You will stop receiving flood warnings if the phone companies no longer provide us with your number. This might happen if:

  • you change or cancel your phone number
  • you move house
  • we change the areas that we provide warnings to

Who your data might be shared with

Third party service providers

We may share your information with our agents or representatives, so they can manage your account. Our agents or representatives will only use your personal data in line with what we have set out in this privacy notice.

We will not:

  • sell or rent your data to third parties
  • share your data with third parties for marketing purposes

Legal obligations

We may share your data or disclose information because of a law, regulation or court order and to protect our interests and legal rights.

How long we keep your data

We will keep your personal data for as long as your account is active, and for 6 years from the date that your flood warnings account is cancelled. This is in line with our retention policy.

We need to keep your details so that we can stop your phone number from being automatically added to the service again after you cancel your account or opt out. After 6 years, we will no longer have a record that you cancelled your account or opted out and your phone number could be automatically added again.

Your rights

A list of your rights under the General Data Protection Regulation, the Data Protection Act 2018 (DPA 2018), is accessible at the Information Commissioner’s Office.

Questions and complaints

Our Data Protection Officer (DPO) is responsible for independent advice and monitoring of the Environment Agency’s use of personal information.

If you have any concerns or queries about how we process personal data, or if you would like to make a complaint or request relating to data protection, please contact:
